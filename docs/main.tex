\begin{abstract}
This paper presents a granular analysis of Spatial Entropy Differential Evolution (SEDE). Unlike traditional metaheuristics that rely on fitness-based diversity tracking, SEDE utilizes an $O(N^2)$ structurally exact Gaussian kernel distance matrix to compute real-time Spatial Shannon Entropy. By coupling this mathematical entropy to dynamic exploitation algorithms via a continuous logistic decay mechanism, SEDE fundamentally mitigates the "Curse of Dimensionality" across 100D landscapes. We validate the C++ OpenMP accelerated variant against standard algorithms (DE, PSO) on complex topological functions and a Real-World Hyperparameter framework.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Global optimization in high-dimensional spaces remains a persistent challenge in computational intelligence \cite{b1}. As dimensionality increases, the search volume expands exponentially, causing standard metaheuristics like Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) \cite{b2} and standard Differential Evolution (DE) \cite{b7} to suffer from "Phenotypic Entropy Paradoxes"—where algorithmic fitness appears converged while the physical swarm coordinates diverge entirely in random high-dimensional spaces.

In this study, we propose Spatial Entropy Differential Evolution (SEDE), a parameter-free framework utilizing raw topological distance matrices and thermodynamic Shannon Entropy to dynamically scale mutation steps. To support production deployment, a high-performance C++ OpenMP back-engine has been coupled to the architecture via PyBind11, scaling massively over traditional Numba integrations.

\section{Proposed Method: SEDE}
The core hypothesis of SEDE asserts that diversity must be structurally tracked regardless of fitness to avoid "Empty Space" phenomena. 

\subsection{Exact Spatial Entropy}
We first define Euclidean distance measurements $d_{ij}$ and subject them to a Gaussian Smoothing Kernel to derive robust spatial densities $\rho_i$:
\begin{equation}
\rho_i = \sum_{j \neq i}^{N} \exp\left( -\frac{d_{ij}^2}{2\sigma^2} \right)
\end{equation}
where $\sigma^2$ is inherently scaled exactly to the mean of non-zero structural distances across the entire active dimensions. The normalized spatial entropy $H_{spatial}$ is derived natively using Shannon's Information theory \cite{b6}.

\subsection{Logistic Mechanism and Evolutionary Cooling}
SEDE replaces standard parameter bounds with mathematically adaptive curves. The Mutation Factor ($F_{new}$) dynamically scales via real-time spatial evaluations:
\begin{equation}
F_{new} = F_{min} + (F_{max} - F_{min}) \times \frac{1}{1 + e^{-k(H_{norm} - H_{mid})}}
\end{equation}
Simultaneously, "Evolutionary Cooling" ensures extreme displacement limits iteratively constrict vectors from traversing excessively outside limits, damping structural displacement strictly from $5\% \rightarrow 0.1\%$ linearly.
